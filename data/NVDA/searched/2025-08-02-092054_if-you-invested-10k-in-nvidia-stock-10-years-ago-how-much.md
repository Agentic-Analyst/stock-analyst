If You Bought Nvidia Stock 10 Years Ago

The company's stock traded at approximately $0.49 per share 10 years ago. If you had invested $10,000, you could have bought roughly 20,408 shares. Currently, shares trade at $173.50, meaning your investment's value could have grown to $3,540,816 from stock price appreciation alone. However, Nvidia also paid dividends during these 10 years.

Nvidia's dividend yield is currently 0.02%. Over the last 10 years, it has paid about $0.19 in dividends per share, which means you could have made $3,878 from dividends alone.

Summing up $3,540,816 and $3,878, we end up with the final value of your investment, which is $3,544,694. This is how much you could have made if you had invested $10,000 in Nvidia stock 10 years ago. This means a total return of 35,347%. In comparison, the S&P 500 total return for the same period was 265.10%.

What Could The Next 10 Years Bring?

Nvidia has a consensus rating of "Buy" and a price target of $181.27 based on the ratings of 38 analysts. The price target implies more than 4% potential upside from the current stock price.

The company on May 28 announced its Q1 2026 earnings, posting revenues of $44.1 billion, up 69% year-over-year and beating the consensus estimate of $43.2 billion.

For Q2, Nvidia sees revenue of $45 billion, plus or minus 2%. The guidance includes the loss of $8 billion in H20 revenue due to the export controls. The company expects gross margins between 71.8% and 72%.
